Self Help Questions

  • What is TB?

    Tuberculosis is caused by bacteria that most often affect the lungs.

  • Is TB curable?

    Yes, TB is curable and preventable.

  • What are the symptoms of TB?

    Symptoms include: blood coughing, Night sweat, Chest pain, Fever, Weight loss etc..

  • Is TB Contagious?

    Yes, TB is highly contagious and can be transmitted from an infected person to an uninfected person.

  • What are the causes of TB?

    It is spread through the air when a person with TB (whose lungs are affected) coughs, sneezes, spits, laughs, or talks.

  • What are the preventive measures for TB?

    Avoiding other people by not going to school or work, or sleeping in the same room as someone, will help to minimize the risk of germs from reaching anyone else. Wearing a mask, covering the mouth, and ventilating rooms can also limit the spread of bacteria.

  • What are the possible complications of TB?

    Meningitis, Spinal pain, Joint damage, Damage to the liver or kidneys, Heart disorders
